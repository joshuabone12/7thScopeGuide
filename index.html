IFV Guide

US Army

Crows

The range finder is in the middle of the crosshair.

The range of the sight is in the 100s of meters with 1 being 100, etc.

US Army

M2A3 Bradley

Rangefinder is circled in green. Shells had just right of the center vertical line, the ranging marks are not where you aim, it's only there for the right elevation of the gun.

(Same Gun and sights on PARS/ACV/ASLAV 25mm)

BAF

FV107

BAF

FV510

CAF

LAV-6/Coyote

For proper adjustment of the gun line the number in meters with its corresponding line.

HE for high explosive, MG for coaxial machine gun, FS for AP rounds.

RGF

BTR-82A

There is no built-in rangefinder for the BTR-82a.

For quick ranging marks use SL markers. Chevrons are off centered on all ammo types with the chevrons automatically changing depending on which type of ammunition is loaded.

RGF

BMP-2

Similar to the previous Russian turrets with the same basic design as the BTR-82a.

The chevrons will change depending on which type of round is loaded. The chevron ranges are in the 100s of meters with SL marks needed for quick rangefinding (BMP2M has rangefinder).

MEA

BMP-1

The left side of the sight is for the HEAT (High Explosive Anti Tank) and the right side is for the fragmentation shell.

The gunner's point of aim is always the center and not offset left or right because of the shell.
